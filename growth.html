Memoirs:

My 7th-grade memoir was hilariously bad. There were details made up, there was practically no imagery, my show don't tell isn't very strong, I had tons upon tons of informal language that didn't fit, and sentence fragments were just scattered through the text. I also didn't try that hard on it, and my proofreading ability was next to none, and I barely edited the passage at all. As a result, I recieved a low grade of a 83

Comparing this to my well-polished memoir from this year, there are already differences that are visible upon first seeing it. For example, there is an entirely true story that happened to me and has no loose ends in it. There are details of imagery and show don't tell used well together that make the reader imagine a true circumstance, and there are barely any conventional. errors throughout the passage. I would consider this one of the best writing passages that I have done through my entire life, and especially compared to my 7th-grade one, it is just completely different and much better, and I ended up getting a 95.
